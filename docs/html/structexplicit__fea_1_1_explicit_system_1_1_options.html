double explicit_fea::ExplicitSystem::Options::damping_alpha

Damping constant α used to construct the proportional damping matrix, Default = 0.01. The damping matrix is formulated from the equation: \( \left[C \right] = \alpha \left[ M \right] + \beta \left[ K \right] \)
where \( \left[ M \right] \) is the global mass matrix, \( \left[ K \right] \) is the global stiffness matrix, and \( \left[ C \right] \) is the proportional damping matrix.

double explicit_fea::ExplicitSystem::Options::damping_beta

where \( \left[ M \right] \) is the global mass matrix, \( \left[ K \right] \) is the global stiffness matrix, and \( \left[ C \right] \) is the proportional damping matrix.
